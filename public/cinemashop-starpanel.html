Safety Protocols

Section 02 • Mandatory
⚠ WARNING — Risk of Electric Shock, Fire, and Injury. Review the assembly instructions to confirm that the appropriate critical components and accessories are used with the furnishing. Compliance with these protocols is not optional.

Before You Begin

  1. Read all instructions and warnings in their entirety before installation. Test panels on the floor prior to mounting.
  2. Ensure proper grounding and that no wiring is exposed.
  3. Install away from heat sources, water, and flammable materials.
  4. Use only the recommended power source. Substitutions are prohibited.
  5. Disconnect the power supply before installing, moving, or servicing the panel.
  6. Handle panels with caution; sharp edges may be present.
  7. Wear gloves, eye protection, and appropriate safety gear.
  8. Cut and trim only with a sharp non-serrated knife or box cutter.
  9. Wear latex gloves to preserve panel cleanliness during handling.

Electrical Safety

  • Always unplug from the electrical outlet before cleaning or servicing.
  • Unplug before installing or removing components.
  • Use this furnishing only for its intended purpose. Do not use unapproved attachments.
  • Never operate with damaged cord or plug, after a drop, or after water exposure.
  • Keep cords away from heated surfaces.
  • Never insert objects into any opening.
  • Do not use outdoors. Indoor dry locations only.
  • Do not operate where aerosol products are in use, or where oxygen is being administered.
  • Ensure wiring meets all local building codes.
  • Disconnect power to the area before beginning installation.
  • Wear protective clothing and eye protection when working with fiber optic components.
  • Inspect for frayed or exposed wires before installation.
  • Never install in damp or wet areas.
  • Plug only into properly grounded outlets.
  • Keep flammable materials away during operation.
  • Never attempt self-repair. Return defective units to a service center.

Emergency Response

Electric Shock: Remove person to fresh air; keep comfortable for breathing. Obtain medical attention.
Skin Cut: Wash with plenty of soap and water. If irritation occurs, obtain medical attention.
Dust in Eyes: Rinse cautiously with water for several minutes. Remove contact lenses if easy to do. Continue rinsing. If irritation persists, obtain medical advice.

Connection Rules

  • Each Starboard Light Engine has 1 IN port and 2 OUT ports. Either OUT may jump to the next starboard.
  • The Master Board provides 3 CAT5 OUTPUT ports.
  • Maximum 9 panels per lead wire. Exceeding this produces data drops.
  • Maximum 27 panels per power supply. Add an additional supply and Master Board beyond this.
  • Each shooting star requires its own dedicated CAT5 lead from the Master Board.
  • The Master Board may live on-panel or remotely in an equipment closet within 150 ft of the first panel.

Routine Cleaning Procedure

Disconnect power before any cleaning. Non-negotiable.
  1. Disconnect AC power to the system.
  2. Allow the system to cool if it has been operating.
  3. Wear clean latex or nitrile gloves to prevent skin-oil transfer.
  4. Use a soft, dry microfiber cloth. Wipe in one direction. Do not scrub.
  5. For dust in crevices, use a soft-bristle brush or vacuum on lowest suction. Hold the attachment above the surface — do not press into the fabric.
  6. Do not use water, solvents, cleaning sprays, or chemical cleaners. Fabric and fiber optics may be damaged.
  7. For stubborn marks, contact CINEMASHOP for approved cleaning agents. Unauthorized chemicals void the warranty.

Fiber Optic Inspection

  • Loose or protruding fibers — reinsert with the supplied poker tool.
  • Discoloration or yellowing — typically indicates aging of an individual fiber.
  • Missing fibers — contact CINEMASHOP for replacement guidance.

Annual Comprehensive Check

  1. Disconnect power.
  2. Power on and cycle through each effect: Twinkle, Firefly, Breeze, Random, Rain Drops, Jamboree.
  3. Cycle through each preset color. Confirm full reproduction without missing channels.
  4. Trigger each shooting star (SS1, SS2, SS3 as installed) at each duration.
  5. Verify the dimmer responds smoothly across 10–100%.
  6. Test ambient light effects and colors if RGB perimeter is installed.
  7. Note any anomalies and consult the troubleshooting section.

Field Cutting Panels

Section 09

Cutting for Protrusions (Sprinkler Heads, Fixture Boxes, HVAC)

  1. As you install the panel, mark the protrusion location on the upper side of the panel.
  2. Remove any fiber optics in the cut area; use the poking tool to reinsert them elsewhere on the panel.
  3. Carefully cut only the 1-inch Fiberglas for the measured opening with a sharp non-serrated knife or box cutter. Do not cut the fabric below.
  4. Make an "X" in the fabric below the opening.
  5. Fold the four resulting fabric flaps up into the opening.
  6. Glue the flaps over the top of the panel using a light adhesive spray such as 3M Super 77.

Cutting for Speakers and Air Vents

For these applications, leave the fabric intact. After cutting only the Fiberglas, reinsert the fiber strands through the fabric.

Trimming Oversized Panels

  1. "Un-wrap" the fabric on the side requiring trimming.
  2. Mark the new cut line on the top of the fiberglass panel.
  3. Remove and relocate any fiber optics in the area.
  4. Cut to the new size with a sharp non-serrated knife or box cutter, taking care not to nick the fabric.
  5. Re-wrap the fabric over the top with slight tension.
  6. Tack the fabric back with a light adhesive spray such as 3M Super 77.
Tool Reminder: Use only sharp non-serrated knives or box cutters for fiber optic panel work. Serrated edges will fray the fabric and damage internal fibers.
