Reading Room

Three flavors of inline explanation: lightweight tooltips, richer popovers, and visual hover cards.

A modern smart home is built around a single whole-home automation system that ties every subsystem together. In the living room, your Dolby Atmos sound stage works in concert with smart lighting and motorized shades to create scenes you can summon by voice. [1]

For dedicated cinema rooms, displays such as the OLED and QLED panels offer different trade-offs in contrast, brightness, and burn-in resistance. For a true private theater, a laser projector paired with an anamorphic lens and a Kaleidescape server delivers reference-grade picture and audio. [2]

Behind the scenes, a properly engineered installation begins with low-voltage rough-in during construction, followed by professional ISF calibration and integration into a unified multi-zone audio system.

Deeper Explanations

Optional reading for clients who want the full technical picture.

What is the difference between integration and automation?

Integration connects devices so they can speak to one another. Automation defines what should happen when one of them speaks. A doorbell that lights up the foyer at night is integration plus automation; a doorbell that simply rings is neither.

Why does cabling matter so much?

Cabling decisions made before drywall determine what your home can do for the next 20 years. Cat6a, conduit, and pre-pulled fiber are inexpensive during construction, expensive afterward, and impossible in some retrofit situations.

What is professional calibration?

Calibration tunes a display or sound system to industry-reference standards using meters, microphones, and test patterns. An ISF-certified technician adjusts grayscale, gamma, color points, and white balance so what you see matches the director's intent.

Most luxury home AV projects fail not because of bad equipment, but because of poor coordination between trades. The single largest predictor of a successful installation is whether the AV integrator was at the table during architectural design.

When AV is designed alongside HVAC and electrical, conduits, ventilation, and acoustic treatment fall into place naturally. When AV is consulted last, the system gets retrofitted into spaces it was never meant to occupy — speakers go where wires can reach instead of where physics says they should be, equipment racks end up in poorly ventilated closets, and remote controls overlap because zones were never properly demarcated. The cost of fixing these decisions later is typically 3–5x the cost of having designed them correctly the first time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers for clients new to home AV integration.

How long does a typical installation take?

A Foundation-tier room can be completed in a single day. A full Reference theater room with new construction typically requires 3–6 weeks, coordinated with the general contractor.

Do I have to choose Control4? Can I use other systems?

No. We work across major automation platforms including Crestron, Savant, and Lutron. Your needs and existing infrastructure inform the choice.

What ongoing support is included?

All installations include a 12-month warranty plus optional service plans with remote monitoring, on-call response, and quarterly tune-ups.

Will my system still work if my internet goes down?

Local control (lights, shades, climate, scenes) continues to function. Remote access and streaming services require internet, but the home itself remains responsive.
