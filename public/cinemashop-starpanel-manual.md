## 1. System Overview and Inventory

Upon receipt of your CINEMASHOP Fiber Optic Star Ceiling Panel system, you shall verify the presence of the following components. I recommend performing this inventory check prior to commencing installation, as the absence of any item may delay your project.

**Standard System Inventory:**

- Acoustical fiber optic panels
- Rotofast anchors (6 per panel required)
- 10-foot low-voltage RJ45 / CAT5 jumper cables
- 30-foot lead conductor flex wire(s) — 4 or 5 conductor configurations — for LED strip lights
- Hex tools and poker tool
- Drywall anchors
- Power supply: 12V at 90 Watts Class 2 (controls maximum 27 panels) OR 24V at 90 Watts (controls maximum 27 panels), with cord connected
- Spacers (optional — used principally when back-lighting panels with LED strips to produce a halo effect on ceiling or wall, or when additional wiring clearance is required)

**Observation:** Occasionally there will be more than one lead wire, and in certain configurations the wire(s) will already be attached to the power supply.

**User-Supplied Items Required:**

- One switchable AC outlet for the 3-prong power transformer cord. Optimal placement is in proximity to AV components in a home theater installation.
- One CAT5 or CAT5e wire run per system. **Important: do not use CAT6 or CAT7 cable.** These cable categories are incompatible with the system.
- Additional CAT5 wires per shooting star: one extra for two shooting stars, two extra (total of three) for three shooting stars.
- Pre-wire from the location where you will plug in the power transformer to the switchable AC outlet.

## 2. Technical Specifications

**Power Consumption Per Panel:**
- Voltage: 12V or 24V DC
- Wattage: 1.4 Watts per starboard

**Panels with Shooting Stars:**
- A panel equipped with a shooting star contains 2 or 3 Star Panel Board light sources.
- This increases panel power by 2.8 Watts, for a total of 4.2 Watts per panel at 12V/24V.

**Power Supply Specification:**
- UL Listed 12V/24V at 90W (UL Listing E530053)
- Maximum interconnected units: 27 panels per power supply
- For installations exceeding 27 panels, an additional 12/24V 90W Class 2 power supply with an additional Master board must be added.
- Class 2 Low Voltage Driver installed in junction box
- Use case: Household use only. Dry location, residential, light duty.

**Daisy Chain Limit Per Lead Wire:**
- Maximum 9 starboards/panels per lead wire.
- Exceeding 9 panels per lead wire will produce data drops — a phenomenon I categorize as suboptimal.

## 4. Pre-Installation Planning

A successful installation is the product of meticulous preparation. I have observed that haste during this phase produces aesthetic and functional defects which are difficult to remediate later.

**Personnel Required:** Two persons minimum, plus a stepladder.

**Step 4.1 — Square the Room**

A room may visually appear square yet not in fact be square. This is an important distinction. Measure twice. All panels align relative to the first panel; therefore, its alignment is critical. A small deviation in the first panel produces a large deviation by the end of the room.

**Step 4.2 — Mark Reference Lines**

Use masking tape, pencil marks, and chalk lines to assist with maintaining square alignment. Locate where your first panel will be installed.

**Step 4.3 — Determine Power Routing Method**

Two options exist. I shall describe both.

> **Option 1 — Remote Driver (Recommended Method):**
>
> The power supply has a prong for line-voltage input. The selected outlet should be controlled by a remote switch or control system. Locate the Driver and Master Board Controller in a proper equipment area that is accessible and within 150 feet of the first star panel.
>
> Connect the Driver to the Master Board Controller via the DC connection. Run a CAT5 cable from one of the RJ45 ports on the Controller to the Star Panel Leader Slave board INPUT ports located on the panel. Begin daisy-chaining the panels using the supplied 10-foot CAT5 jumper cables. Consult the Star Panel manufacturer if a longer leader cable is required.

> **Option 2 — Embedded Transformer / Power Supply:**
>
> If the power supply cannot be installed plugged into an existing receptacle in the occupied space, contact a licensed electrician to embed the Driver into the ceiling. This may be necessary in older homes and retrofits where running new low-voltage wire is not feasible. The Driver is a line-voltage device and most electrical codes require access; therefore a cutout in the panel will be necessary. Use the optional Cut Out Kit to mark and cut. Observe local building codes.

**Critical Pre-Installation Tip:** After completing the power and lead wire runs, test every panel before mounting it by plugging the lead wire into the Star Panel Master Board Controller. Identifying a defective panel prior to mounting is, by my calculations, significantly more efficient than diagnosing it after installation.

## 5. Step-by-Step Installation Procedure

**Step 1 — Measure and Snap Ceiling Grid Lines**

This is the most important phase of installation. Using laser measurements, determine the ceiling centers and snap those primary grid lines. Then measure and snap lines for each individual ceiling panel. These must be exact, as they indicate where the Rotofast anchors will be placed.

**Step 2 — Understand the Mounting Hardware**

The basic mounting components are: a drywall anchor, a screw, a spacer, and a Rotofast. The procedure is as follows:

1. The drywall anchor is screwed into the drywall.
2. The Rotofast and spacer are placed onto the screw.
3. The assembly is screwed into the anchor.
4. The hex head of the Rotofast is snapped into place, permitting the hex wrench to operate it from below the panel.

**Step 3 — Verify Measurements and Mark Mounting Points**

Rotofast anchors are 2 inches in dimension, providing a large surface area and high pull-out strength. They are designed to fasten 6 lb/ft³ rigid fiberglass core acoustical panels to any substrate.

Mark each panel with Sharpie pen circular markings 4 inches from each edge in the corners, and at the center of each outside edge. This produces six total mounting points per panel.

**Important: Only six Rotofast fasteners are required per panel.** The placement is: four corners (4 inches inset) plus one at the center of each outside edge.

Measure twice. Snap once. This adage is, in my analysis, statistically validated.

**Step 4 — Test Panel Electronics**

Prior to lifting any panel into position, connect the power cable and confirm the panel illuminates correctly. Testing while the panel is at floor level is significantly easier than diagnosis post-installation.

**Step 5 — Lift and Position the First Panel**

This step requires two people. Begin from the center of the ceiling and work outward toward the walls. Tilt the panel slightly, lift it above the framework, and let it settle into place.

**Useful Tip:** A paint brush extension (available at most hardware stores) may be employed to hold the panel in place while a second person engages the fasteners.

The panel face is marked with masking tape at the Rotofast positions — 4 inches from each corner and at the center edge. Panels are somewhat fragile. Proceed slowly.

**Step 6 — Engage the Corner Rotofasts**

Once the panel is fully positioned and aligned, the Rotofasts can be engaged.

1. Insert the hex wrench through the face of the panel at the mark.
2. Use the ice pick (poker tool) to locate the exact position of the hex nut within the Rotofast.
3. Insert the hex wrench into the hex nut.
4. Rotate **counter-clockwise** to screw the Rotofast into the panel material, raising the panel toward the ceiling.
5. Apply light pressure while turning to help seat the fastener.
6. Rotate **clockwise** to lower the panel.

**Caution:** Turn slowly. Rotofasts can be over-rotated, which compromises their hold.

**Step 7 — Level the Panel**

Repeat the Rotofast engagement at each of the four corners while a second person holds the panel in position. The goal is a panel face level with the edging strip and with adjacent panels. Apply gentle pressure combined with slow hex wrench rotations.

**Step 8 — Engage the Center-Edge Rotofasts**

After the four corners are secured, engage the two center-edge Rotofasts to level the middle sections of the panel.

**Step 9 — Repeat for Subsequent Panels**

Continue the procedure for each remaining panel, working outward from the center. Verify level alignment with previously installed panels at every step. A smooth, continuous surface is the desired outcome.

**Field-Cutting Panels for Obstructions:**

When a panel must accommodate a protrusion (such as a sprinkler head, fixture box, or HVAC penetration):

1. As you install the panel, mark the protrusion location on the upper side of the panel.
2. Remove any fiber optics in the cut area and use the poking tool to reinsert them elsewhere in the panel.
3. Carefully cut only the 1-inch Fiberglas for the measured opening, using a sharp non-serrated knife or box cutter. Take care not to cut the fabric beneath.
4. Make an "X" in the fabric below the opening.
5. Fold the four resulting fabric flaps up into the opening.
6. Glue the flaps over the top of the panel using a light adhesive spray such as 3M Super 77. This creates a finished edge.

**Field-Cutting for Speakers and Air Vents:**

For these applications, leave the fabric intact. After cutting only the Fiberglas, reinsert the fiber strands through the fabric.

**Field-Cutting Oversized Panels:**

When a panel is slightly larger than the installation area:

1. Un-wrap the fabric on the side requiring trimming.
2. Mark the new cut line on the top of the fiberglass panel.
3. Remove and relocate any fiber optics in the area.
4. Cut to the new size with a sharp non-serrated knife or box cutter, taking care not to nick the fabric.
5. Re-wrap the fabric over the top with slight tension.
6. Tack the fabric back with a light adhesive spray such as 3M Super 77.

## 6. Wiring and Power Configuration

The CINEMASHOP system permits multiple wiring topologies. I shall describe the principal configurations.

**Basic Daisy Chain Topology:**

The Master Board Controller may be located either on a panel or in a closet. From the Controller, run a CAT5 cable to the INPUT of the first panel's Starboard Light Engine. From that panel's OUTPUT, run another CAT5 jumper to the next panel's INPUT. Continue daisy-chaining.

Each Starboard Light Engine has one IN port and two OUT ports. You may use either of the two OUTPUT ports to jump to the next starboard. The redundant output permits branched routing.

**Daisy Chain Maximum:** 9 panels per lead wire. Exceeding this quantity will result in data drops.

**Multi-Branch Topology:**

The Master Board Controller contains three CAT5 OUTPUT ports. Each output may serve a separate daisy chain of up to 9 panels, yielding up to 27 panels per Master Board.

**Shooting Star Configurations:**

A shooting star is a special starboard which traverses one or more panels. Configurations include:

- **One shooting star per panel** — installed entirely within a single panel
- **Two cross-panels** — the shooting star spans two adjacent panels
- **Three cross-panels** — the shooting star spans three panels

Each shooting star requires its own additional CAT5 lead wire from the Master Board.

**Multiple Master Boards:**

If your installation exceeds 27 panels, additional Master Boards with their own 12/24V 90W Class 2 power supplies must be installed. Master Boards interconnect via their INPUT/OUTPUT TO MASTER ports.

**RGB / RGBW Perimeter Strip Lights (Halo Effect):**

For perimeter strip lighting:

1. An additional ILLUMINATE 1.0 RGB Strip Light Controller is required.
2. Power the RGB Controller from the same 12V 90W supply (or a dedicated supply).
3. Connect the RGB Controller to the Master Board via RJ45/CAT5 (power and communication).
4. Connect the LED Strip Output of the RGB Controller to the LED strips via the 4 or 5 conductor flex wire.
5. RGB strip lights are configured as a single area or zone per controller.

## 7. ILLUMINATE 1.0 Control System Setup

The system may be controlled via Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, or RS232. I have observed the application functions on both iOS and Android platforms.

**Initial App Setup:**

1. Download the ILLUMINATE 1.0 application from the Apple App Store or Google Play Store.
2. Activate Bluetooth on your mobile device. If Bluetooth is not active, the application will prompt you to enable it.
3. Launch the application. Proceed to adjust settings as desired.

You may switch between Android, Apple, and RS232 control at any time.

**Star Ceiling Controls:**

The "Stars" section of the application controls the star ceiling itself. Available controls include:

- **Starry Night Effects** — animated patterns: Twinkle, Firefly, Breeze, Random, Rain Drops, Jamboree
- **Starry Night Colors** — preset palette plus custom color wheel
- **Shooting Star** — independent control of SS1, SS2, SS3 with color and interval selection (Off, 1 min, 2 min, 5 min, 10 min)
- **On/Off Toggle** — turns stars on or off
- **Dimmer** — adjusts brightness from 10% to 100%

**Ambient Light Controls (Halo Strip Lights):**

The "Ambient Light" section controls the perimeter RGB/RGBW strip lights. Available controls include:

- **Ambient Light Effects** — Fade, Blink, No Effect
- **Ambient Light Colors** — preset palette plus custom color wheel (with hue and opacity adjustment)
- **Speed Slider** — controls the effect speed
- **Dimmer** — adjusts brightness from 10% to 100%
- **On/Off Toggle** — turns ambient lights on or off

**Confirmation Behavior:** Upon selecting a show or color, the system blinks twice to confirm the command before commencing the show. This is a normal diagnostic behavior, not a fault.

## 8. RS232 Command Reference

For installations integrated with home automation control systems, RS232 commands provide direct programmatic control. All command strings terminate with the asterisk character `*`.

**Starry Night Effects (single character):**

| Effect | Command |
|---|---|
| Twinkle | T |
| Firefly | F |
| Breeze | B |
| Random | R |
| Rain Drops | P |
| Jamboree | J |

**Starry Night Colors** — Format: `c,R,G,B,W*`

| Color | RGB+W Value | Command |
|---|---|---|
| Red | 255,0,0,0 | c,255,0,0,0* |
| Orange | 255,50,0,0 | c,255,50,0,0* |
| Amber | 255,150,0,0 | c,255,150,0,0* |
| Yellow | 255,255,0,0 | c,255,255,0,0* |
| Green | 0,255,0,0 | c,0,255,0,0* |
| Light Green | 0,255,150,0 | c,0,255,150,0* |
| Lime | 0,255,50,0 | c,0,255,50,0* |
| Aqua | 0,255,255,0 | c,0,255,255,0* |
| Blue | 0,0,255,0 | c,0,0,255,0* |
| Violet | 150,0,255,0 | c,150,0,255,0* |
| Purple | 255,0,200,0 | c,255,0,200,0* |
| Pink | 255,0,50,0 | c,255,0,50,0* |
| White | 0,0,0,255 | c,0,0,0,255* |
| Warm White | 202,218,255,0 | c,202,218,255,0* |
| Natural White | 255,246,237,0 | c,255,246,237,0* |
| Cool White | 255,193,141,0 | c,255,193,141,0* |

**Color Transitions:**

| Effect | Command |
|---|---|
| Wipe color | w |
| Fade color | f |
| Rainbow color | r |
| Blink color | b |

**Star Dimmer** — Format: `D,%*` (range 10 to 100)

Example: 80% dim = `D,80*`

**Shooting Stars** — Format: `Zone,R,G,B,W,Time*`

Zones:
- Zone 1 (SS1) = X
- Zone 2 (SS2) = Y
- Zone 3 (SS3) = Z

Time values:
- Off = 0
- 1 minute = 1
- 2 minutes = 2
- 5 minutes = 5
- 10 minutes = 10

Example commands:

| Function | Command |
|---|---|
| Zone 1 red, 1 minute | X,255,0,0,0,1* |
| Zone 2 blue, 10 minutes | Y,0,0,255,0,10* |
| Zone 3 green, 2 minutes | Z,0,255,0,0,2* |
| Off SS1 | X,0,0,0,0,0* |
| Off SS2 | Y,0,0,0,0,0* |
| Off SS3 | Z,0,0,0,0,0* |

**Ambient Light Effects** — Format: `mode,speed` (speed range: 150 = slow, 255 = fast)

| Effect | Command |
|---|---|
| Fade | e,speed |
| Blink | k,speed |
| No Effect | n |

Examples: `e,255` = fade fast; `e,150` = fade slow; `k,255` = blink fast.

**Ambient Light Colors** — Format: `A,R,G,B,W*` (identical color values to Starry Night Colors, but prefixed with `A` rather than `c`).

**Ambient Light Dimmer** — Format: `d,%` (range 10 to 100)

Example: 80% dim = `d,80`

## 9. Maintenance Protocol

Although the system is designed for minimal maintenance, periodic inspection and cleaning will preserve optimal function and aesthetic quality.

**Recommended Frequency:**

- **Visual inspection:** Every 6 months
- **Light dust removal:** Every 3 to 6 months, or as needed
- **Connection verification:** Annually
- **Power supply inspection:** Annually

**Routine Cleaning Procedure:**

1. **Disconnect power to the system** prior to any cleaning operation. This is non-negotiable.
2. Allow the system to cool if it has been operating.
3. Wear clean latex or nitrile gloves to prevent oils from your skin transferring to the panel fabric.
4. Use a soft, dry microfiber cloth to gently wipe the panel surface. Move in one direction. Do not scrub.
5. For dust accumulation in panel crevices, use a soft-bristle brush or a vacuum cleaner equipped with a soft brush attachment on its lowest suction setting. Hold the attachment slightly above the panel surface — do not press into the fabric.
6. Do not use water, solvents, cleaning sprays, or chemical cleaners directly on the panels. The fabric and fiber optics may be damaged.
7. For stubborn marks, contact CINEMASHOP for approved cleaning agents. Unauthorized cleaning chemicals void the warranty.

**Fiber Optic Inspection:**

Periodically inspect the visible fiber endpoints for:

- Loose or protruding fibers — these may be reinserted with the supplied poker tool.
- Discoloration or yellowing — typically indicates aging of an individual fiber.
- Missing fibers — contact CINEMASHOP for replacement guidance.

**Power Supply Inspection:**

- Verify the power supply is free of dust accumulation. Use a dry cloth or compressed air (held upright, not inverted) to remove debris.
- Confirm the power supply remains in a dry location with adequate ventilation.
- Inspect the lead wire for cracks, kinks, or damage. Replace if compromised.
- Verify the AC plug is firmly seated in a grounded outlet.

**Master Board and Controller Inspection:**

- Verify all RJ45 connections are firmly seated.
- Inspect cables for kinks, sharp bends, or pinch damage.
- Verify status indicator LEDs (if visible) illuminate normally.

**Mobile Application Updates:**

- Periodically check the App Store or Google Play Store for ILLUMINATE 1.0 application updates.
- Updated firmware (if pushed by the manufacturer) may improve stability or add features.

**Storage of Spare Parts:**

If you possess spare panels, jumper cables, or Rotofast anchors, store them in a clean, dry, climate-controlled location away from direct sunlight. Sunlight may degrade the fiber optic materials over extended periods.

**Annual Comprehensive Check:**

Once per year, I recommend performing the following:

1. Disconnect power.
2. Power the system back on and cycle through each effect (Twinkle, Firefly, Breeze, Random, Rain Drops, Jamboree). Confirm each functions correctly.
3. Cycle through each preset color. Confirm full color reproduction without missing channels.
4. Trigger each shooting star (SS1, SS2, SS3 as installed) at each available duration.
5. Verify the dimmer responds smoothly across the full 10–100% range.
6. Test all ambient light effects and colors if RGB perimeter lighting is installed.
7. Note any anomalies and refer to the troubleshooting section.

## 10. Troubleshooting Diagnostic Guide

I have organized common faults by symptom. If a fault is not resolved by these procedures, contact CINEMASHOP technical support.

### 10.1 — No Stars Illuminate at All

**Probable Causes and Resolutions:**

1. **AC power is absent.**
   - Verify the AC outlet is energized. Test with another device.
   - Verify the switchable outlet is in the "On" position. This is a common oversight.
   - Confirm the power supply cord is firmly seated in the outlet and the power supply.

2. **Power supply has failed.**
   - Verify the power supply indicator (if present) illuminates.
   - Inspect for visible damage or burned components.
   - If failed, replace with the manufacturer-specified 12V/24V 90W Class 2 unit only.

3. **DC connection between Driver and Master Board is loose or absent.**
   - Verify the DC barrel connector is fully inserted into the Master Board.

4. **Application not paired.**
   - Open the ILLUMINATE 1.0 app. Confirm Bluetooth is enabled on your mobile device.
   - Confirm the on/off toggle in the "Stars" section is set to On.
   - Confirm the dimmer is not set below 10%.

### 10.2 — Some Panels Illuminate, Others Do Not

**Probable Causes and Resolutions:**

1. **Daisy chain exceeds 9 panels per lead wire.**
   - Count the panels on the affected chain. If more than 9, the system will exhibit data drops. Redistribute panels across additional Master Board outputs, or add an additional Master Board and power supply.

2. **Defective CAT5 jumper between functional and non-functional panels.**
   - Identify the last working panel. The fault is in the cable between it and the next panel, or in that next panel.
   - Substitute a known-good CAT5 jumper cable. If panels then function, the original cable is defective.

3. **Loose RJ45 connector.**
   - Disconnect and firmly reseat the RJ45 connector at the IN port of the first non-functional panel. Listen for an audible click.
   - Inspect for bent pins inside the RJ45 jack.

4. **Wrong cable category used.**
   - Verify all jumpers and the lead wire are CAT5 or CAT5e. **CAT6 and CAT7 will not function correctly.**

5. **Defective starboard light engine.**
   - If a single panel fails to illuminate while panels before and after it function correctly, the panel's light engine may be defective. Contact CINEMASHOP for warranty service.

### 10.3 — Shooting Star Does Not Function

**Probable Causes and Resolutions:**

1. **Shooting star duration set to Off (0).**
   - Open the application. Set the desired duration (1, 2, 5, or 10 minutes).

2. **Missing dedicated CAT5 lead for the shooting star.**
   - Each shooting star requires its own CAT5 from the Master Board. Verify the appropriate number of leads is connected.

3. **Wrong zone selected in the application.**
   - Confirm SS1, SS2, or SS3 corresponds to the physically installed shooting star configuration.

### 10.4 — Mobile Application Will Not Connect

**Probable Causes and Resolutions:**

1. **Bluetooth not enabled on the mobile device.**
   - Open device settings. Enable Bluetooth.

2. **Mobile device out of range.**
   - Bluetooth range is typically 30 feet. Approach the Master Board.

3. **Another mobile device already connected.**
   - Only one device may control the system at a time. Disconnect from the other device.

4. **App not updated.**
   - Update the ILLUMINATE 1.0 application via the App Store or Google Play.

5. **Master Board in fault state.**
   - Disconnect AC power to the system for 30 seconds. Reconnect. Attempt reconnection.

### 10.5 — Stars Flicker or Behave Erratically

**Probable Causes and Resolutions:**

1. **Power supply is overloaded.**
   - Count total connected panels and shooting stars. Verify total wattage does not exceed 90W per power supply.
   - Each standard panel: 1.4W. Each panel with shooting star: 4.2W.
   - If overloaded, add an additional power supply and Master Board.

2. **CAT5 cable run length excessive.**
   - The first leader CAT5 should not exceed 150 feet from Master Board to first panel. Standard jumpers are 10 feet each.

3. **Electromagnetic interference.**
   - Verify CAT5 runs do not pass parallel to high-voltage AC wiring within close proximity. Cross AC wiring at right angles where possible.

4. **Loose connections.**
   - Reseat all RJ45 connectors along the affected chain.

### 10.6 — Color Reproduction Is Incorrect

**Probable Causes and Resolutions:**

1. **Wrong color selected.**
   - Verify the application color selection. Try the custom color wheel and adjust hue and opacity.

2. **Damaged conductor in lead wire.**
   - The LED strip flex wire has 4 or 5 conductors. A break in one conductor will eliminate one color channel. Replace the lead wire.

3. **Defective RGB Strip Light Controller.**
   - If only ambient lights exhibit incorrect color, the RGB controller may be faulty. Contact CINEMASHOP.

### 10.7 — RS232 Commands Are Ignored

**Probable Causes and Resolutions:**

1. **Command not terminated with asterisk.**
   - All RS232 commands require the closing `*` character. Verify your command string.

2. **Incorrect command syntax.**
   - Refer to Section 8 of this manual. Verify the exact format for the intended function.

3. **Serial parameters mismatch.**
   - Verify your home automation system is configured for the expected baud rate and serial parameters. Consult CINEMASHOP if these specifications are required.

4. **RS232 cable defective or miswired.**
   - Substitute a known-good cable. Verify pinout.

### 10.8 — Panel Sags or Is Loose

**Probable Causes and Resolutions:**

1. **Rotofast over-rotated.**
   - Excessive rotation strips the fastener's hold in the panel material. Replace the affected Rotofast.

2. **Insufficient mounting points engaged.**
   - All 6 Rotofasts (4 corners plus 2 center-edges) must be engaged. Verify all are seated.

3. **Drywall anchor failure.**
   - The drywall may be deteriorated at the anchor location. Reposition the anchor to firmer drywall, or use a larger anchor rated for the load.

### 10.9 — Visible Light Leaks Between Panels

**Probable Causes and Resolutions:**

1. **Panels not properly leveled.**
   - Use the hex wrench to make fine adjustments at each Rotofast. Rotate counter-clockwise to raise, clockwise to lower. Aim for a smooth, continuous surface across panel edges.

2. **Ambient room light bleeding past panel edges.**
   - Verify perimeter trim is installed. Add light-blocking trim if necessary.

### 10.10 — A Fiber Optic Strand Has Come Loose

**Probable Causes and Resolutions:**

1. **Use the supplied poking tool** to reinsert the loose fiber into the panel.
2. If multiple fibers are loose or the panel face is damaged, contact CINEMASHOP for warranty evaluation.
